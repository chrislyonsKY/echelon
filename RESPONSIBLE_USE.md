# Responsible Use

## Purpose

Echelon is intended for lawful GEOINT and OSINT research, journalism, academic analysis, crisis monitoring, maritime awareness, and humanitarian or public-interest investigation.

This project is not designed, licensed, or maintained for harmful targeting, unlawful surveillance, or offensive operations.

## Allowed Uses

Examples of acceptable use include:

- monitoring publicly observable conflict or maritime activity
- researching infrastructure, logistics, and event corroboration from open data
- academic or journalistic analysis of open-source events
- humanitarian situational awareness
- defensive security or resilience analysis using openly available information

## Disallowed Uses

You must not use Echelon for:

- targeting individuals or groups for physical harm
- unlawful surveillance, stalking, harassment, or doxxing
- facilitating offensive military, paramilitary, or intelligence operations
- persecuting people based on ethnicity, nationality, religion, politics, gender, or similar protected characteristics
- evading source-provider access restrictions or terms of service
- presenting unverified outputs as authoritative intelligence without appropriate caveats

## Project-Specific Standards

Because Echelon blends AI outputs, imagery, and OSINT feeds, users and contributors must:

- treat convergence scores as indicators, not proof
- preserve provenance and attribution
- distinguish between observation and interpretation
- avoid overstating confidence from a single source family
- respect source licensing and redistribution limits

## AI and Copilot Use

The copilot is an analyst aid, not an analyst. Its outputs must not be used as sole justification for decisions that could materially affect people, infrastructure, or safety.

Contributors should avoid adding prompts, tools, or workflows that encourage:

- identity claims without corroboration
- target nomination
- autonomous or quasi-autonomous harmful action
- suppression of provenance or uncertainty

## Imagery and Evidence

Open imagery and evidence handling must remain careful and contextual:

- graphic or disturbing content should be handled deliberately
- scene interpretation should not be presented as fact without supporting context
- non-commercial imagery restrictions must be honored

## Enforcement

Maintainers may reject contributions, remove content, or block participation when project spaces or features are used to promote or facilitate harmful or unlawful activity.

Use of the software remains subject to the repository license, disclaimer, code of conduct, and security policy, but maintainers reserve the right to set stricter community standards for participation.
