# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors, maintainers, and community members pledge to make participation in the Echelon project a respectful, professional, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, technically rigorous, and constructive community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for this project include:

- Being respectful, direct, and professional in technical discussion
- Assuming good faith while still challenging weak reasoning, unsafe patterns, or poor evidence
- Giving and receiving feedback in a concrete and constructive way
- Focusing code review and discussion on the work, not the person
- Respecting attribution, licensing, and provenance requirements for OSINT, imagery, and third-party data
- Using the project responsibly and discussing misuse risks seriously

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or discriminatory language
- Personal attacks, trolling, inflammatory behavior, or repeated bad-faith argument
- Publishing someone else's private or identifying information without explicit permission
- Knowingly submitting malicious code, credential leaks, malware, or exploit material unrelated to legitimate defensive research
- Using project spaces to promote unlawful surveillance, targeting, doxxing, or violence
- Misrepresenting source provenance, evidence confidence, or licensing terms

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take fair, appropriate action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples include GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, social posts tied to the project, and direct communications about project collaboration.

## Reporting

To report Code of Conduct violations, email **conduct@chrislyons.dev** or **security@chrislyons.dev** if the matter also involves safety, abuse, or security concerns.

Include, when possible:

- What happened
- Where it happened
- Links or screenshots if relevant
- The GitHub usernames or handles involved
- Any immediate safety concerns

Reports will be reviewed as promptly and discreetly as practical. Maintainers will respect the privacy and security of reporters to the extent possible.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may apply any action they believe is appropriate to the situation, including:

- Private warning
- Request to stop specific behavior
- Comment or content removal
- Temporary suspension from project spaces
- Permanent ban from project participation

Severe cases involving threats, stalking, extortion, credential theft, malware, or attempts to facilitate unlawful harm may be escalated immediately without prior warning.
